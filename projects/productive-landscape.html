01 — M. Arch Thesis

Productive Landscape

Type M. Arch Thesis
Location TU Dublin
Year 2025
Program Landscape · Infrastructure · Production
Description

The thesis investigates the relationship between productive landscape, food infrastructure, and architectural form in the Irish midlands. Beginning with an analysis of the bog landscape around Lackeen Castle, the project develops a territorial strategy along the River Shannon — one that integrates working land, processing infrastructure, and public space into a single architectural proposition.

The design asks what it means to make architecture from the logic of a productive landscape: its cycles, its materials, its seasonal rhythms. The buildings that result are defined by what the land produces — timber, earth, stone — and by the labour that moves through them.
